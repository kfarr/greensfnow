Treat Plaza North

Transforming a contested sewer construction site into a community green space in San Francisco's Mission District

üå± Project Overview

Treat Plaza North represents a unique opportunity to transform a site of infrastructure controversy into a community asset. Over the past two years, extensive community outreach has demonstrated overwhelming neighborhood support for transforming this underutilized space into a green infrastructure asset that integrates with the SFPUC's Folsom Area Stormwater Improvement Plan. Through dozens of community events, workshops, and door-to-door conversations, residents have consistently expressed enthusiasm for creating permeable green space that manages stormwater while providing much-needed community gathering space.

üéØ Project Goals

  • Stormwater Management: Install bioswales and permeable surfaces to reduce runoff
  • Community Space: Create a gathering place for neighborhood events and activities
  • Urban Heat Reduction: Add trees and vegetation to cool the urban environment
  • Air Quality: Improve local air quality through increased vegetation
  • Safe Streets: Implement traffic calming measures and improved pedestrian safety
  • Biodiversity: Introduce native plants to support local wildlife
