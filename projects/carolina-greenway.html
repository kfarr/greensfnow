Carolina Greenway

A safe, green walking route for schoolchildren and community members from Potrero Hill to the heart of the Showplace Design District — reconnecting neighborhoods with a continuous corridor of native plants, street trees, biodiversity habitat, and bioswale stormwater infrastructure.

Take Action to Support This Project

📍 Location

The Carolina Greenway is a continuous corridor spanning four connected segments:

  • Carolina St, Mariposa to 17th — fronting the to-be-renovated Jackson Park
  • Carolina St, 17th to 16th — an unaccepted street block with significant greening potential
  • Carolina St, 16th to 15th — currently near-zero tree canopy or greening
  • 15th St, Carolina to De Haro — the connection to the forthcoming Live Oak school annex

Neighborhoods: Potrero Hill ↔ Showplace Square / Design District
Adjacent: Jackson Park, Live Oak School (current + future annex), Potrero Hill Montessori, Showplace Square Design District, former Anchor Brewing properties

🌱 Project Vision

The Carolina Greenway is a neighborhood-scale vision of a continuous green route that gives Potrero Hill's kids and families a safe, shaded, biodiverse walk down the hill and into Showplace Square and vice versa. Live Oak students will be able to walk from the main campus at Mariposa and Arkansas, through a renovated Jackson Park, down the Carolina Greenway, and across 15th Street to the new Live Oak annex — all under tree canopy, alongside native plants, with traffic calmed and stormwater captured in bioswales.

The greenway stitches together four very different blocks — a park frontage, an unaccepted street, a treeless corridor, and a school-annex connector — into a single, coherent pedestrian-first spine linking Potrero Hill to the Design District.

🌳 Why This Corridor

  • Safe route for kids: Gives Live Oak and Potrero Hill Montessori students a continuous, traffic-calmed walking route from home and school
  • Hill-to-Design-District connection: Provides Potrero Hill residents a green pedestrian spine into the heart of the Showplace Design District
  • Fills a canopy gap: Carolina between 15th and 16th is nearly devoid of street trees or greening today — a high-impact place to add canopy and habitat
  • Unaccepted street opportunity: Carolina between 16th and 17th is unaccepted, creating design flexibility without standard street-acceptance constraints
  • Park-adjacent multiplier: Carolina from Mariposa to 17th already benefits from the planned Jackson Park extension (6 feet into Carolina, 200 feet from Mariposa) — a natural anchor for the greenway
  • Biodiversity & native plants: A continuous corridor supports pollinators and wildlife far better than isolated plantings
  • Stormwater management: Bioswales and permeable surfaces along four blocks capture and filter meaningful runoff volumes
  • Excess street width: Carolina is wider than its traffic needs require; through traffic is minimal and most use is parking-related
