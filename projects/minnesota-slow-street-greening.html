Minnesota Slow Street Greening

Turning Dogpatch's beloved MinneSLOWta corridor — a permanent Slow Street running from Mariposa to 22nd — into a lush, tree-lined community greenway with rain gardens, street trees, and community-art corners, stewarded in partnership with the Dogpatch & Northwest Potrero Hill Green Benefit District.

📍 Location

Corridor: Minnesota Street from Mariposa Street to 22nd Street
Neighborhood: Dogpatch / Potrero Hill, San Francisco
District: Supervisor Shamann Walton (District 10)
Key Connections: Esprit Park, Progress Park, Warm Water Cove, Museum of Craft and Design, Minnesota Street Project art gallery, UCSF Mission Bay, protected bike lanes on 4th and 16th Streets, Central Waterfront businesses

🌱 Project Overview

Minnesota Slow Street — affectionately known as MinneSLOWta — runs through the heart of Dogpatch, safely connecting parks, museums, businesses, and residents, and providing a vital low-stress alternative to 3rd Street for people walking, biking, scooting, and rolling. Originally implemented as a COVID-19 Response Slow Street in 2020, the SFMTA Board approved Minnesota as a permanent Slow Street on December 6, 2022, and the corridor's long-term design was approved at a January 5, 2024 public hearing.

The 2024 design adds a northbound diverter at 19th Street, a painted safety zone and median island at 18th, painted safety zones at 18th and 19th that can host community art, a new traffic calming island between 20th and 22nd, and Slow Streets pavement markings and wayfinding throughout. These painted zones and islands — plus the corridor's generous right-of-way and existing community stewardship — make Minnesota a ready canvas for the next step: permanent, beautiful, ecologically productive greening.

Minnesota Slow Street Greening envisions a gradual transformation from painted interventions and plastic posts into a living greenway: street trees, rain gardens, native plantings in the traffic calming islands, community-designed street murals in the painted safety zones, and permanent planters at daylighted corners — all maintained in partnership with the Dogpatch & Northwest Potrero Hill Green Benefit District.

🏗️ Planned Improvements

  • Street Trees: New street trees along the Minnesota corridor to provide canopy, shade, and habitat — filling gaps where the existing streetscape is bare
  • Rain Gardens & Sidewalk Gardens: Capture stormwater from the corridor and surrounding Dogpatch blocks before it reaches the combined sewer, reducing runoff to Islais Creek and the Bay
  • Greened Painted Safety Zones: Transform the new painted safety zones at 18th and 19th Streets into living corners — native plantings, permanent planters, and community-designed street murals in the remaining painted areas
  • Landscaped Traffic Calming Island: Plant the approved traffic calming island between 20th and 22nd with drought-tolerant native plantings instead of bare concrete or paint
  • Greened Diverter at 19th: Soften the northbound diverter island at Minnesota & 19th with plantings, turning a traffic control feature into a neighborhood landmark
  • Permanent Planters at Daylighted Corners: Replace plastic soft-hit posts at daylighting zones with durable planters, hardening safety improvements while beautifying the street
  • Community Art & Wayfinding: Extend the beloved Dogpatch Planet Walk and other community art installations along the corridor, paired with Slow Streets wayfinding
  • GBD-Stewarded Maintenance: All new plantings maintained by the Dogpatch & NW Potrero Hill Green Benefit District, unlocking permanent greening that many SF projects struggle to sustain

🎯 Project Goals

Short-Term: Activate & Harden the Approved Design

  • Planter Pilot: Place movable and permanent planters at the new painted safety zones and daylighted corners on 18th and 19th as SFMTA implements the 2024 design
  • Community Art Corners: Partner with MinneSLOWta and neighborhood artists to paint street murals in the painted safety zones — continuing the tradition of the Annual Spooky Slow Street Stroll and Dogpatch Planet Walk
  • Tree Planting Events: Work with Friends of the Urban Forest and DPW BUF to plant new street trees along the corridor with neighborhood volunteers

Medium-Term: Pursue Funding & Permits

  • Pursue SFMTA Slow Streets bulb-out funding, SFPUC Green Infrastructure programming, and Community Corners plan installations for permanent concrete & plantings
  • Coordinate with the Dogpatch & NW Potrero Hill GBD to fold new greening into their ongoing maintenance scope

Long-Term: A Permanent Dogpatch Greenway

  • Bulb-Outs with Plantings: Permanent concrete bulb-outs planted with trees and shrubs at key intersections along Minnesota
  • Connected Rain Garden Network: A corridor-length network of sidewalk rain gardens capturing Dogpatch stormwater before it reaches the Bay
  • Model Slow Street Greenway: Minnesota becomes a citywide model for how neighborhoods with an active Green Benefit District can fully transform a Slow Street into a permanent greenway

📈 Current Status

Slow Street Status: Permanent (SFMTA Board approved December 6, 2022)
Design Phase: Full design package approved at January 5, 2024 public hearing — includes diverter at 19th, painted safety zones at 18th and 19th, median island at 18th, traffic calming island between 20th and 22nd, and Slow Streets wayfinding
Implementation: Pending SFMTA installation
Greening Vision: Building community support for permanent greening of the approved painted safety zones, islands, and corridor — in partnership with the Dogpatch & NW Potrero Hill Green Benefit District
