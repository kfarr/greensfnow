Cole Valley Plaza

Transforming a successful pop-up urban forest experiment into a permanent community green space at the intersection of Carl, Cole, and Clayton Streets

üå± Project Overview

Cole Valley Plaza represents an opportunity to transform a successful pop-up experiment into permanent green infrastructure. Through three pop-up Town Square events in 2025, this awkward wedge of asphalt where the N Judah veers off Carl Street has been transformed into a lush urban forest with over 500 plants, demonstrating the community's enthusiasm for converting underutilized road space into vibrant public gathering areas. The project aims to bring nature to a neighborhood that's heavy on gorgeous Edwardian homes but light on public open spaces.

üéØ Project Goals

  • Public Open Space: Create much-needed green gathering space in dense Cole Valley
  • Urban Forest: Install permanent trees and vegetation to improve air quality and reduce urban heat
  • Community Building: Provide outdoor venue for neighborhood events and daily socializing
  • Safe Streets: Create natural traffic calming and improved pedestrian safety
  • Entertainment Zone: Continue partnership with Cole Valley Nights monthly market
  • Stormwater Management: Incorporate permeable surfaces and bioswales where feasible

üéâ Pop-Up Success Story

The temporary pop-up events have demonstrated overwhelming community support:

  • Three successful Pop-Up Town Square events in 2025 (April, May, and June)
  • Each event featured 500+ plants delivered by Flora Grubb Gardens via 18-wheeler
  • Hundreds of residents of all ages participated in activities including Hula-Hooping, pizza, jugglers, and musical performances
  • Created safe play space for children with natural plant barriers
  • Integrated with Cole Valley Nights monthly market as part of Entertainment Zone
  • Next pop-up (June 12) may remain in place for 96 hours with overnight security

As gardening pro Flora Grubb noted: "It's literally countercultural now to just leave the house and do something together outside. It's bizarre that that's where we've landed."
